You have a Kidney Disease!

Please take the following actions:

Dos:
  1. Stay Hydrated: Do drink plenty of water as advised by a healthcare professional.
  2. Choose Fruits and Vegetables: Opt for kidney-friendly options like apples, berries, and leafy greens.
  3. Incorporate Whole Grains: Include whole grains like brown rice and quinoa for fiber.
  4. Opt for Lean Proteins: Consume lean sources of protein like chicken, fish, and legumes.
  5. Use Healthy Fats: Choose healthy fats like olive oil over saturated fats.
  6. Limit Salt Intake: Season your food with herbs instead of salt to manage blood pressure.
  7. Plan Balanced Meals: Aim for a balanced plate with appropriate portions.
  8. Consider Plant-Based Proteins: Try beans and lentils in moderation to reduce animal protein intake.
Don’t:
  1. Avoid High-Sodium Foods: Don’t consume processed foods high in salt.
  2. Limit Potassium-Rich Foods: Don’t overconsume foods high in potassium if advised by a healthcare provider.
  3. Steer Clear of Phosphorus-Rich Foods: Don’t eat foods high in phosphorus like processed cheeses and cola.
  4. Limit Red and Processed Meats: Don’t consume excessive amounts which can strain the kidneys.
  5. Avoid Sugary Foods and Beverages: Don’t consume high-sugar foods as they can increase kidney strain.
  6. Minimize Alcohol Intake: Don’t drink alcohol excessively as it can worsen kidney function.
  7. Avoid Overeating: Don’t overeat, as excess weight increases kidney disease risk.
  8. Limit Dairy Products: Don’t consume high amounts of dairy if sensitive to phosphorus.
Suggested Medications Commonly Used in CKD: Should Take Under Doctor Coonsultation
  1. Lisinopril or Losartan: Lowers blood pressure and protects kidney function.
  2. Furosemide: A diuretic that reduces fluid buildup, easing pressure on the kidneys.
  3. Epoetin Alfa: Stimulates red blood cell production to address anemia from CKD.
  4. Sevelamer: A phosphate binder to control high phosphate levels.
  5. Calcitriol: A Vitamin D supplement for bone health.

Proper doctor consultation needed.
